Personal transportation ideas to reduce the carbon footprint in Belding

Personal transportation has been a huge upgrade that people have been seeing in the big cities.

Public bikes and scooters are all over the main cities such as Grand Rapids and their accesibility is really easy, with one touch of a button from a app you can rent a scooter and leave it wherever you want when you are done.

So we think that this type of transporation would be an incredible addition to Belding to help reducing our carbon footprint.

How are we going to implement these ideas in Belding

Implementing Bikes in Belding

Public bikes are the main transportation update the city will be able to implement. We took the price of $200 each and we thought 50 bikes spread across 10 locations would be an adequate amount.

Belding has a small downtown area that is easy to navigate on a bicycle and also a highway that has much of the population and businesses on or near it, so bikes are an ideal mode of transportation.

Each night we would pay a person for collecting the bikes and replacing them in their original racks for the beginning of the day. The bikes can be repaired and maintained at the bike shop in Greenville and any simple repairs can be done in the city.

Implementing Scooters in Belding

Along with what we are going to do with bikes we are going to also implement around 50 scooters over the main areas in Belding.

We are thinking of contacting companies such as Lime to give us scooters, the good thing about Lime is with their mobile application anyone would be able to rent the scooters and after that pay for the amount of time they used the scooter for.

The average cost for a scooter is about $300 and the maintenance is depending on the damage, so it ranges is $50-$200.

Where to put trails and bike paths in Belding

One problem that we might face when implementing the personal transportation systems is the fact that we don't have either bikes or scooters paths or trails. So in order to fix that problem we will need some money to paint bike paths on the roads and or to make trails for bikes.

But because our idea is to use the bikes and scooters as a tranportation method, we'd just need to paint paths on the roads which won't be really expensive.

The main area where we are going to create bike paths is the downtown Belding area, close to the High Scholl and where all the restaturants and gas stations are located.
